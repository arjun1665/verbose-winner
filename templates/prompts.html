Daily Writing Exercises
5-Minute Sprint

Write continuously for 5 minutes without stopping. Don't worry about grammar or structure - just let your thoughts flow.

Observation Exercise

Look around you. Pick one object and write a detailed description using all five senses.

Word Association

Take a random word and write a short story that must include: the word, a character with a secret, and something red.

Story Starters

"The last thing I expected to find in my grandmother's attic was..."

"Every night at exactly 11:47 PM, the same stranger walks past my window..."

"The email arrived from an address I didn't recognize: my own, from 10 years in the future..."

Character Development Prompts
Backstory Builder

Create a character who has changed their name three times. What were they running from each time?

Relationship Dynamics

Write about two characters who can't stand each other but must work together to achieve a common goal.

Internal Conflict

Develop a character whose greatest strength is also their greatest weakness.

World Building Exercises
Setting Deep Dive
  • What does this place smell like?
  • What sounds are always present?
  • Who holds power here and how do they maintain it?
  • What's forbidden in this society?
  • What do people do for entertainment?
Historical Layers
  • What event shaped this place the most?
  • What ruins or remnants tell old stories?
  • What traditions have been lost?
  • Who are the legendary figures?
  • What's the biggest lie people believe about their history?
Making the Most of Writing Prompts
Set a Timer

Give yourself a specific time limit. This prevents overthinking and encourages flow.

No Editing

Don't edit while writing from a prompt. Focus on getting ideas down first.

Embrace the Unexpected

Let the prompt take you somewhere you didn't plan to go. Some of the best ideas come from surprises.

Build on Success

If a prompt sparks something great, save it and develop it into a larger piece.
